Students are the ones paying the price.

We aren't just paying for a degree; we are paying for a functional system. Small percentages at scale mean missed emails, delayed feedback, and students left without support. That's not good enough — and it's fixable. These commitments focus on taught programmes and address the systems failures that sit between students and staff.

02 — My Platform

Three commitments
to students.

Where I have executive authority, I will act directly. Where academic governance approval is required, I will bring time-bound proposals and publish outcomes.

1

The Problem

End the "Feedback Lottery"

Feedback is inconsistent, late, and often uninformative — with no warning when deadlines are missed. Computer Science: only 48% of students say feedback helps them improve, almost 30% below the university average.

A. University-Wide Standard

  • Feedback returned within the published deadline or flagged in advance
  • Feedback must reference assessment criteria and include actionable guidance
  • Token or one-sentence responses will not meet the standard

B. Transparency Clause

  • If a deadline will be missed, students notified before the deadline
  • A revised date must be provided
  • Departments report compliance each term
  • Aggregate data published by department
% assessments returned on time % late with advance notice Student-reported usefulness
2

The Problem

Fix the Timetabling Mess

Late timetable changes make it impossible to plan work, care, and study. Music Production: 45% of students not satisfied with course organisation.

A. 4-Week Publication Rule

  • All teaching times and locations published at least 28 days in advance
  • Any change inside this window requires a logged justification
  • Exception rates reviewed every term
  • Evidence it can be done: University of Essex (2026), University of Hertfordshire (2025)

B. Automated Change Alerts

  • Any room or time change triggers an automatic email the moment it is logged
  • SMS alerts for unavoidable changes
  • Persistent issues escalated to Faculty leadership
% sessions changed inside 4 weeks Average notice period Sessions affected per term
3

The Problem

Make the PAT System Visible

Students don't know who their Personal Academic Tutor is or how to contact them. This is a systems failure — not a people problem.

A. PAT Integration

  • PAT name and contact details displayed prominently on the student VLE landing page
  • Simple fix to a systems failure
  • Departments report PAT contact rates

B. Semesterly Check-In

  • Every student has confirmed academic contact with their PAT each semester
  • In-person, online, or documented academic exchange
  • Students confirm completion — responsibility shared
  • Non-compliance feeds into annual programme review

03 — Delivery

A plan with
real deadlines.

This isn't a list of vague intentions. These are time-bound deliverables with published outcomes.

First Term

Foundations in place

  • Timetabling rules and automated alerts implemented
  • PAT integration completed on student VLE landing page
  • Feedback standards published for consultation

By End of Academic Year

Full accountability active

  • Feedback standards enforced across all departments
  • Transparency Clause active — missed deadlines must be notified in advance
  • Compliance data published termly, by department
  • PAT semesterly check-in confirmed for all students

Ongoing

Visible, accountable, public

  • All compliance data published and accessible to students
  • Repeat breaches escalated through programme review
  • Annual review of all three commitments against OfS benchmarks
